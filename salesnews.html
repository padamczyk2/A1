During multiple COVID lockdowns, we have been exposed to more webinars than ever before. Our minds have been bombarded with digital information, all of which promising to be a glimpse of a better future or a magic key, unlocking your professional potential. More of our business activities have moved to a digital reality as well. With an ever-increasing need for data storage and management, digital and cloud services have skyrocketed.
Brave sky sailors have flown to share some of the best tips on how to fine-tune your Digital & Cloud Services sales with some IT consulting, magenta style. By adapting some of these techniques into your client conversations, you will help to broaden our image from your local telco provider to a future-forming digital data leader. Rather than a client conversation, we’d like to call them consulting sessions. Compelling arguments backed up by our clients’ success stories are concrete. Best part - you don’t have to make anything up! So what is the best way to start?
1. PREPARATION
Before talking to the client, grab a coffee with an Account Manager who’s assigned to other services provided for them and ask away. Find out what they can share about client’s priorities. Are there any clues for where this company’s digitalization journey might lead? Getting an idea of how the client’s competitors go about their businesses could be a great starting point for a conversation with another Digital & Cloud Services expert. Our talented ninjas will surely share a few tips that might just make you feel more secure with the technical aspect of things. Reach out via contact form connected to our ‘On Cloud 9’ newsletter.
2. CONVERSATION
Already done some digging? Time to deep-dive into client’s big blue. A good starting point is to find out how COVID lockdown has influenced the ways of working in the organization. Were there any IT difficulties with work from remote locations? Another anchor point is data. How does a customer store it? Data can be supported by technologies that make it even more valuable. Carefully chosen dashboards support its management in real-time, giving your client new outlooks on expanding their services and revenue. The third point of reference is what we will call ‘application comfort’ and its connection to Digital & Cloud Services. Is there any software that feels old-fashioned and needs a refresh? Presenting technological trends and benefits of further stages of digitalization journey might just do the trick. From there it’s not far to our product portfolio. Ideally, we want our encouraged consumer to feel excited and consider introducing new products to their workflow. Be mindful not to push this part of the conversation too hard, though.
3. ASSURANCE
During the conversation, keep in mind you are a part of Deutsche Telekom, a partner in telecommunication with decades of experience and reliability. Our international experience speaks for itself.
